The Brown Bear

About the Brown Bear

The Brown bear (Usrsus Arctos) is native to parts of nothern Eurasia and North American. It's conservation status is currently Least Concern.

There are many subspecies within the Brown Bear species, including the Atlas Bear and the Himalayain Bear.

Species

  • Arctos
  • Collarus
  • Horribilis
  • Nelsoni (Extinct)

Features

Brown Bear are not always comepletly brown. Some can be reddish or yellowish. They have very large, curved claws and huge paws. Male Brown Bears are often 30% larger than female Brown Bears. They can range from 5 feet to 9 foot from head to toe.

Habitat

Countries with Large Brown Bear Population

  1. Russia
  2. United States
  3. Canada

Countries with Small Brown Bear Population

Some countries with smaller Brown Bear populations include Armenia, Belarus, China, Finland, France, Greece, India, Nepal, Poland, Romania, Slovenoa, Turkmenistan, and Uzbekistan.
